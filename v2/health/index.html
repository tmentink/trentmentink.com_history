BMR

No matter what you are doing, your body is constantly burning energy. The term Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) refers to the number of calories your body needs to perform its daily functions like breathing and the beating of your heart. Your BMR is the most important number for creating your personalized diet plan. Luckily you can estimate it with a simple calculation.

Weight Goals

In the previous section, you estimated the total amount of calories your body burns on a daily basis. Now depending on your weight goals, you will either add or subtract from that number. I highly encourage you to not get greedy and add or subtract more than 20% of your total daily calories expecting to see faster results. This will only shock your body and could lead to health concerns. The key to achieving your goal is consistency and patience. Macros

In this section you will learn how to convert calories into macronutrients. There are three major macronutrients that the body needs: fats, protein and carbohyrdates. One gram of fat is equal to 9 calories while one gram of protein/carbs is equal to 4 calories. Knowing this will allow you to calculate how many grams of fat, protein and carbs you need to eat on a daily basis. In the form below I have set up some ratios that you can use for estimates. Everyone's body is different so I reccommended that you experiment with different ratios until you find something that works best for you.
